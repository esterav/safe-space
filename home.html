This treatment has been developed to be conducted under the supervision of a psychotherapist, who will be in the room at all moment and will determine whether the user is likely to be benefited by this treatment or not, and the number of session he/she will need. In order to get the best results, there should be an introductory relaxation period that will prepare the patient for the immersion. After that, the journey begins:

INTRODUCTORY PERIOD
Before starting the virtual reality treatment, there will be 2/3 sessions destined to prepare the patient for the experience, providing information about the questionnaire they have to fulfil and the gradual immersion.
POST-TREATMENT
After the VR exposure sessions accorded with the therapist, the patient will be able to follow up the relaxation sessions using the key element for a positive visualisation.


5/10 minutes of exposure (depending on the needs of the patient, whether there is going to be a guided meditation or not).

It will start presenting the audio first. Image will fade in and the user will be asked  to connect all the senses to the exposure.

During the exposure the user will be asked to try to locate the place visited and to focus in one element.

VISUALISATION TREATMENT
I. Relaxation. In order to provide the best results, the patient should be located in a comfortable place, either sit or laid down. The individual will be asked to breath deeply few times in order to relax his body. After this, he will be asked to put the VR headset on. Now the experience begins.
II. Questionnaire. The first part of the process consist in a series of questions, regarding 7 items (context, environments, colours, artificial elements, therapeutical elements and audio) that will allow the platform to collect data from the user and develop the most accurate computer generated image due to his/her needs.
III. Visualisation. The second part of the process is the immersion into the environment.
