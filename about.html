the vr visualisation treatment.
This VR visualisation treatment provides an immersion to a relaxation environment created by means of an image generated program. As a part of a psychological therapy to treat patients diagnosed with generalised anxiety disorder, this technique is intended to replace some traditional visualisation practices for VR exposures.
Two contexts play a key role in this process: the visual and the auditive. The purpose of this practice is not only to induce the relaxation, but also to sharpen the senses and the memory, generating a connexion between the fictional environment and places already visited. Hyperrealistic natural environments and ambient sounds are essential to revisit the sensations and emotions experienced in those moments, and, in time, make easier the reconstruction of these safe spaces without any tech support.  
To provide the best possible user experience, these environments are developed in a 360 format, readable through any virtual reality headset. But first, in order to create the bespoke atmosphere is necessary to be familiar with the status of the patient and his/her lever of concentration and relaxation. The first step in the creation process is to identify the patient’s personality through a questionnaire that will analyse the response of the user towards four variables (visual, auditive and thermic sensory characteristics, relation with the environment, level of activity and personality). This items are considered relevant to get to know the psychological characteristics of the patient that will enable the emocional induction.  
how visualisation plays an important role in relaxation?
Visualisation is a tool widely used in enhancing relaxation because it gives the user the power to create their own spaces and, therefore, be in charge of their own emotions.

Brain and body have a very strong bond. Using guided visualisation can trigger a feeling in the individual that can be interpreted by all senses. For example, visualising yourself in your favourite beach on the earth not only involves the image, but also hearing the sound of the waves crashing on the sand, feeling the heat  of the sun on the skin and the characteristic salty smell of the sea.
The power of providing all these feelings with the imagination relies on the ability of each person to be very accurate in the reconstruction and his/her concentration.

Another important aspect about visualisation is personalisation. It can deliver bespoke environments, for all types of minds and personalities. The more accurate the visualisation, the better response. Training the mind to visualise places already visited aims, in time, to be able to induce a positive emotion in the body by only reconstructing the image.
for who is addressed?
People who is diagnosed of General Anxiety Disorder, which is “is a condition in which the patient experiences persistent, excessive and intrusive worrying to the extent that daily functioning becomes difficult.”
People punctuated with a high level of neuroticism and responsibility.
The use of the VR in treating anxiety has always been seen as a support to a traditional practice. A study about “the use of virtual reality technology in the treatment of anxiety” analyses the advantages of using VR for exposure treatment. The most important advance is the use of the technology for breaking the barrier between envisioning a place and experiencing it. It comes to be a very useful tool for those who experience trouble using the imagination. The complete control of the exposure makes it easier to manage and adapt the scenarios to each individual (compared to revisiting a place or creating a simulation in real life) and to manipulate them in order to encourage a gradual immersion.
